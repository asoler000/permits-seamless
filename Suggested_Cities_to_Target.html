Suggested Cities to Target

Priority Markets for Permit-Driven Intelligence

Prepared by Adriana Soler for Seamless FM

Executive Summary: These 15 markets represent the highest-value opportunities for permit-driven maintenance intelligence. Prioritization is based on permit data accessibility, chain restaurant/retail density, construction activity, HVAC criticality, and vendor network viability.

Tier 1 Markets — Start Here (Months 1-3)

1. Dallas-Fort Worth, Texas (Score: 45/50)

  • #1 in commercial construction permit volume nationally
  • Fort Worth: Fastest permit processing in US
  • Part of highest fast-food density corridor in America
  • 100°F+ summers = HVAC mission-critical
  • Strong permit data accessibility

Why start here: Highest signal volume + accessible data + critical HVAC market

Primary targets: QSR chains, retail chains, casual dining

2. Austin, Texas (Score: 44/50)

  • Top 6 for permit data API access
  • Fast-growing tech + service economy
  • Hot climate drives continuous HVAC demand
  • High commercial renovation activity

Why target: Best permit data access in Texas + strong growth

Primary targets: Tech-forward restaurants, modern retail concepts

3. Miami, Florida (Score: 43/50)

  • Top 3 in construction permit volume
  • Florida = #1 state for HVAC employment
  • Hot/humid climate = HVAC is survival not optional
  • Dense retail + restaurant market

Why target: HVAC criticality + massive chain presence

Primary targets: Resort/hotel restaurants, coastal retail, QSR chains

4. Orlando, Florida (Score: 43/50)

  • #1 fast food penetration in America (1,058 residents per QSR)
  • Highest chain restaurant concentration nationally
  • Tourism + hospitality = massive facility maintenance market
  • Year-round hot/humid climate

Why target: Highest chain density anywhere + tourism means can't afford downtime

Primary targets: Theme park restaurants, hotel F&B, highway corridor retail

5. Phoenix, Arizona (Score: 44/50)

  • Top 3 construction permit activity
  • 120°F summers = HVAC failure means immediate closure
  • Strong retail expansion market
  • Good permit data systems

Why target: Most HVAC-critical market in America

Primary targets: Desert retail, QSR chains, strip mall restaurants

Tier 2 Markets — Expand Next (Months 4-6)

6. Chicago, Illinois (38/50)

Excellent permit API, dense urban market, may require relationship building

7. Houston, Texas (39/50)

High chain density, hot/humid climate, large commercial market

8. Atlanta, Georgia (40/50)

Top 5 construction, Southeast retail hub, distribution center for national brands

9. Nashville, Tennessee (39/50)

Tennessee #1 for permit efficiency, growing restaurant market, RFMA connections

10. Seattle, Washington (38/50)

Top 5 construction volume, excellent data access, strong retail market

Tier 3 Markets — Strategic Expansion (Months 7-12)

  • 11. Los Angeles, California — Massive market, permit API available
  • 12. San Francisco, California — Premium market, excellent data access
  • 13. Tampa, Florida — Southeast retail strength, HVAC-heavy climate
  • 14. New York City — Largest market, high complexity
  • 15. Charlotte, North Carolina — Emerging market, strong growth

Launch Roadmap

Month 1-2: Pilot

  • Dallas-Fort Worth ONLY
  • Build data pipeline, prove signal quality

Month 3: Validate

  • Add Austin + Miami OR Phoenix
  • Confirm system scales

Month 4-6: Expand Tier 1

  • Complete all 5 Tier 1 markets
  • Optimize rep workflow

Month 7-12: Add Tier 2

  • Systematic rollout of secondary markets

Key Insights

  • Start narrow (1 city) before expanding
  • Hot climate markets = higher HVAC signal quality
  • Chain concentration matters more than population size
  • Data accessibility is non-negotiable
