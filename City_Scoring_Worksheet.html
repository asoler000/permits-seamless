City Scoring Worksheet

Prepared by Adriana Soler for Seamless FM

How to use this worksheet: Evaluate any city as a potential market for the permit-driven intelligence system. Score each criterion from 1-10, add them up, and use the interpretation guide to determine priority level.

The 5 Scoring Criteria

Criterion 1: Permit Data Accessibility (1-10)

  • 10: Public API, real-time data (Austin, Chicago, Seattle, SF)
  • 8-9: Online portal, weekly updates (Dallas, Phoenix)
  • 6-7: Online portal, monthly updates (Miami, Atlanta)
  • 4-5: Manual requests required
  • 1-3: Must visit in person

Criterion 2: Chain Restaurant/Retail Density (1-10)

  • 10: Highest concentration nationally (Orlando)
  • 8-9: Very high chain presence (Dallas, Miami, Phoenix)
  • 6-7: Solid chain presence (Houston, Atlanta, Nashville)
  • 4-5: Moderate presence
  • 1-3: Few chains, mostly independent

Criterion 3: Construction Activity Volume (1-10)

  • 10: Top 3 nationally (Dallas-Fort Worth, Miami, Phoenix)
  • 8-9: Top 10 nationally (Seattle, Atlanta, Austin, Nashville)
  • 6-7: Strong regional market (Houston, Charlotte, Tampa)
  • 4-5: Moderate activity
  • 1-3: Low volume

Criterion 4: HVAC Criticality (1-10)

  • 10: Extreme heat/humidity, HVAC failure = closure (Phoenix, Miami)
  • 8-9: Hot summers, critical 6+ months (Dallas, Austin, Orlando, Houston, Atlanta)
  • 6-7: Moderate climate, important (Seattle, SF, Portland)
  • 4-5: Mild climate
  • 1-3: Cool climate, heating more important

Criterion 5: Vendor Network Viability (1-10)

  • 10: Strong existing network
  • 8-9: Adjacent to current operations
  • 6-7: Buildable, contractors available
  • 4-5: Limited vendor pool
  • 1-3: Not viable
